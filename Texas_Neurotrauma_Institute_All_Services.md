## NCV-EMG

Nerve Conduction Studies (NCV) & Electromyography (EMG)

Advanced Testing for Nerve and Muscle Disorders

Healthy nerves and muscles work together to control movement, sensation, and coordination. When nerve damage or muscle dysfunction occurs, it can lead to symptoms such as numbness, tingling, weakness, pain, muscle cramps, or difficulty with movement. Nerve Conduction Studies (NCV) and Electromyography (EMG) are advanced diagnostic tests that evaluate the health and function of the peripheral nerves and muscles to help identify the underlying cause of these symptoms.

At Texas Neurotrauma Institute, our specialists perform comprehensive NCV and EMG testing to accurately diagnose a wide range of neuromuscular conditions. These studies provide valuable information that guides treatment decisions, supports rehabilitation planning, and helps patients receive timely, targeted care.

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment. NCV and EMG testing provide the answers needed to restore function and improve quality of life.

Our NCV & EMG Services

Our comprehensive diagnostic evaluations help identify disorders affecting the nerves, muscles, and neuromuscular system.

Nerve Conduction Studies (NCV) – Measuring the speed and strength of electrical signals traveling through peripheral nerves to detect nerve damage, compression, or dysfunction.

Electromyography (EMG) – Evaluating the electrical activity of muscles to identify disorders affecting the muscles or the nerves that control them.

Peripheral Neuropathy Evaluation – Assessing nerve damage associated with conditions such as diabetes, traumatic injuries, infections, and other neurological disorders.

Entrapment Neuropathy Testing – Diagnosing nerve compression conditions such as carpal tunnel syndrome, cubital tunnel syndrome, and other peripheral nerve entrapments.

Neuromuscular Disorder Assessment – Evaluating muscle weakness, muscle pain, and neuromuscular conditions to support accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.

Nerve Injury & Radiculopathy Evaluation – Helping identify nerve root injuries, spinal nerve compression, and other conditions affecting movement, sensation, and reflexes.

Advanced Diagnostic Technology – State-of-the-art NCV and EMG equipment that delivers precise, reliable neuromuscular assessments.

Experienced Neurological Specialists – Clinicians skilled in diagnosing complex nerve, muscle, and neuromuscular disorders through expert interpretation of test results.

Comprehensive Neurological Care – NCV and EMG findings are integrated with neurological examinations, imaging studies, and other diagnostic evaluations to develop a complete understanding of each patient's condition.

Our Approach

At Texas Neurotrauma Institute, we believe that identifying the source of nerve and muscle dysfunction is essential to achieving the best possible outcomes. Our NCV and EMG evaluations are performed with precision, interpreted by experienced specialists, and integrated into a comprehensive treatment strategy tailored to each patient's unique needs.

## Electroencephalography

Electroencephalography (EEG)

Advanced Brainwave Testing for Accurate Neurological Diagnosis

An Electroencephalogram (EEG) is a safe, painless, and non-invasive diagnostic test that records the brain's electrical activity. By measuring brainwave patterns, EEG helps physicians evaluate neurological function and identify abnormalities that may be associated with seizures, epilepsy, altered consciousness, sleep disorders, and other neurological conditions.

At Texas Neurotrauma Institute, we utilize advanced EEG technology as part of a comprehensive neurological evaluation. Our experienced specialists perform detailed brainwave assessments to support accurate diagnosis, guide treatment decisions, and monitor neurological health over time.

Accurate diagnosis begins with understanding the brain's electrical activity. EEG provides critical information that helps us deliver precise, individualized neurological care.

Our Electroencephalography (EEG) Services

Our EEG services are designed to evaluate brain function and assist in diagnosing a wide range of neurological conditions.

Routine EEG Testing – Recording brainwave activity to identify abnormal electrical patterns that may indicate neurological disorders.

Extended & Ambulatory EEG Monitoring – Long-duration monitoring for patients whose symptoms require observation over an extended period during normal daily activities.

Seizure & Epilepsy Evaluation – Assessing abnormal brainwave activity to assist in diagnosing seizure disorders and guiding treatment decisions.

Diagnostic Support for Neurological Conditions – EEG testing may be recommended as part of the evaluation of unexplained episodes of altered consciousness, fainting, confusion, sleep disturbances, or other neurological symptoms.

Treatment Monitoring – Follow-up EEG studies to evaluate changes in brain activity and help monitor the effectiveness of ongoing treatment when clinically appropriate.
